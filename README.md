# Subspace.money — Product Teardown
**Product Intern Assignment · May 2026**

---

## About the Company

Subspace.money is a Bengaluru-based consumer fintech platform founded in 2021 by Ashok Kumar and Mritunjoy Dash (IIT Madras alumni). It operates at the intersection of savings, subscription management, and group finance — letting users split OTT and SaaS subscriptions with others, buy discounted gift cards, and rent gadgets with 10-minute delivery in select cities.

What makes Subspace genuinely interesting is the numbers: ₹36.5 Cr ARR in FY25, bootstrapped, profitable, and a team of under 5 people. For a company that size, that revenue figure is exceptional. The product is rated India's No.1 Savings and Sharing App on the Play Store (3.5★ across 3,200+ reviews) and has no serious active competitor in its core vertical.

The teardown focuses on the shared subscription model — where users join group plans for Netflix, Spotify, Canva, and 100+ other services at up to 80% off. This is Subspace's most defensible, most differentiated, and most competitively empty product. It is also where the most fixable problems live.

---

## Approach

I used the app and website as a real user — explored the shared subscription flow, read through the Play Store review history, checked the website homepage and onboarding, and mapped the competitive landscape independently.

Each of the 5 feedbacks follows the required structure:

- **(a) Observed** — specific screen, flow, copy, or behaviour I actually saw
- **(b) Problem** — the real user or business pain behind it
- **(c) Ship instead** — a specific, actionable solution

The 5 feedbacks are spread across all required pillars: GTM & ICP, Competitor Analysis, Features/Services, UX, and Potential Collaborations.

## The Central Argument

All five feedbacks point to the same root issue: **Subspace does not yet know what it is.**

The product has a genuinely strong core — the shared subscription model is cheap to operate, sticky by design, and has no real competition in India. But the GTM is diluted, the feature hierarchy buries the best product, the UX borrows identity cues from the wrong category (quick-commerce), and the collaboration strategy is non-existent.

The fix is not to build more features. It is to focus ruthlessly on the shared subscription model, clean up the trust layer that is currently generating the most negative reviews, and let the marketing and partnerships flow from that cleaner product experience.

Subspace has a window. The Netflix password-sharing crackdown since 2023 has pushed millions of Indian users toward needing exactly the kind of structured, trusted group plan infrastructure Subspace offers. No competitor of any scale is in this space right now. The window is real — but it will not stay open forever.
