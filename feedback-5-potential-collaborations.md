# Feedback #5 — Potential Collaborations
**Subspace.money · Product Teardown · May 2026**

---

## (a) Observed

Subspace currently has no visible brand partnerships, no co-marketing integrations, and no publicly announced collaborations with OTT platforms, telecom providers, payment apps, or educational institutions. The product page, website footer, and app store listing show zero partner logos, zero co-branded offers, and zero mentions of any institutional tie-up.

Every deal on the platform — Netflix, Spotify, Canva — is facilitated by Subspace independently, without any formal arrangement with those platforms. There is no UPI deep-link, no integration with PhonePe or Google Pay for group payment collection, and no campus programme listed anywhere.

For context — India's OTT market in 2025 is dominated by JioHotstar (~14 crore subscribers), Amazon Prime Video (~6 crore), and Netflix (~4 crore). Plans range from ₹149–₹649 per month. None of these platforms offer a native group-split or shared-plan infrastructure for unrelated users outside a single household. That gap is exactly where Subspace lives — and it is currently navigating it entirely alone, without leveraging any of the distribution, trust, or network advantages that a well-chosen partner could bring.

---

## (b) Problem

A platform that enables group financial transactions between strangers is fundamentally a trust product. The single biggest barrier to Subspace's growth is not awareness — it is the trust gap. A new user considering joining a shared Netflix plan with a stranger on the internet needs to feel safe. Right now, Subspace is asking users to extend that trust based entirely on its own brand, which is young, small, and still associated with the admin abuse complaints visible in its Play Store reviews.

The right collaborations would solve this in a way that years of organic brand-building cannot. A UPI payment partner like PhonePe brings transaction credibility. A campus network brings a pre-verified, tightly-knit user base where social accountability already exists. An OTT platform tie-up brings legitimacy to the shared plan model itself.

Without any of these, Subspace is growing purely on word of mouth in a space where trust is the product — which is slow, fragile, and vulnerable to a single viral negative experience.

---

## Four Collaboration Tracks — Prioritised by Impact

### Track 1 · Highest Impact — Campus Networks (IITs, NITs, private engineering colleges)

Partner with student bodies and hostel committees at Tier-1 colleges. The hostel is the natural unit for a shared plan — 4 people in a wing splitting Netflix at ₹120/month each instead of ₹649.

Appoint one student per campus as a "Subspace Campus Admin" who manages group plans for their batch. This creates a trusted, accountable admin (their real identity and social reputation is on the line) instead of a stranger, directly fixing the admin trust problem that drives the most negative reviews.

**Why it works:** Low CAC, high retention, built-in social accountability. Requires zero technology integration and zero negotiation with large companies — just a Google Form and a WhatsApp group.

---

### Track 2 · Trust Multiplier — UPI Payment Integration (PhonePe or Google Pay)

Build group payment collection natively via UPI. Right now users pay Subspace via whatever payment method is available, but there is no co-branded, deep-linked experience.

A PhonePe integration where a group admin sends a "Subspace Split Request" directly through PhonePe — and members pay in one tap — would remove the friction of manual coordination, reduce payment defaults within groups, and bring PhonePe's brand trust into the transaction.

PhonePe already handles ~47.8% of India's UPI transaction volume and has 600M+ users. Even a lightweight referral integration here is a distribution play that money cannot buy directly.

---

### Track 3 · Long-Term Moat — OTT Platform Tie-ups (SonyLIV, ZEE5, regional platforms)

Netflix and Spotify are not going to formally partner with a platform that facilitates sharing of their plans — their entire crackdown strategy is against exactly this. But second-tier OTT platforms have the opposite problem: low subscriber penetration and a genuine need for new user acquisition channels.

SonyLIV (~2–3M paid subs), ZEE5, Aha, Hoichoi, Sun NXT — these platforms would benefit from Subspace bringing them group subscribers at reduced CAC. A formal revenue-share partnership where Subspace is an official distribution channel for these platforms' group plans would give Subspace legitimacy, give the OTT platforms growth, and give users a plan that is unambiguously legal and supported.

---

### Track 4 · Distribution Play — CRED Store or Similar Rewards Ecosystem

CRED's user base is premium, high-credit-score, urban Indians — a segment that already pays for multiple subscriptions and would immediately understand Subspace's value.

A listing on CRED Store as a featured offer (*"Split your Netflix — pay ₹120/month instead of ₹649"*) would expose Subspace to a high-intent, high-trust audience without any paid acquisition spend. CRED's model already revolves around unlocking deals for its users, making Subspace a natural fit for its rewards inventory.

---

## (c) Ship Instead

Start with Track 1 — the campus network. It requires zero technology integration, zero partnership negotiation with large companies, and zero marketing spend. The output is a cohort of verified, socially accountable admins who have real-world relationships with their group members — which directly solves the biggest trust problem in the product today.

Run a pilot at 5–10 colleges in Bangalore and Chennai (where Subspace's Bengaluru base gives it proximity). Measure admin quality scores, group retention, and payment default rates versus the general platform average. If the numbers are better — which they will be — use the data to pitch Track 2 and Track 3 partners.

No large company will co-brand with Subspace until Subspace can show clean trust metrics. The campus programme is how you earn those metrics.

**The sequencing matters:**

1. Fix the trust layer via campus networks
2. Use clean metrics to unlock a UPI integration
3. Use the UPI integration's transaction data to approach OTT platforms with a formal distribution pitch

Each collaboration builds the credibility needed to land the next one. Done in reverse — going to OTT platforms first without a clean trust story — will get Subspace ignored or rejected. Done in order, this is a compounding flywheel that no competitor can replicate quickly because it is built on real relationships and real data, not just a product feature.
